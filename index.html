Password Security Best Practices

Use Unique Passwords

Never reuse passwords across multiple accounts. Each service should have its own unique, strong password.

Enable Two-Factor Authentication

Add an extra layer of security by enabling 2FA on all your important accounts whenever possible.

Update Passwords Regularly

Change your passwords periodically, especially for critical accounts like banking and email.

Use a Password Manager

Password managers help you store and generate strong passwords securely without memorizing them.

Avoid Public WiFi for Sensitive Activities

Don't enter passwords on public WiFi networks. Use a VPN or wait for a secure connection.

Watch for Phishing Attempts

Always verify the website URL before entering your password. Be suspicious of unexpected login requests.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it safe to use an online password generator?

Yes, our password generator is completely safe. All passwords are generated locally in your browser using JavaScript. We don't store, transmit, or have access to any passwords you generate.

How long should my password be?

We recommend at least 12 characters for most accounts, and 16+ characters for highly sensitive accounts like banking or email. Longer passwords are exponentially harder to crack.

Should I include special characters in my password?

Yes, including special characters significantly increases password strength. However, make sure the service you're using supports the special characters you choose.

How often should I change my passwords?

Change passwords immediately if you suspect they've been compromised. For regular updates, every 3-6 months is reasonable for important accounts, though using strong, unique passwords is more important than frequent changes.
